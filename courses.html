Undergraduate Courses

An undergraduate course, otherwise referred to as a bachelor’s degree tends to be between three and four years long. However, there are some technical course that can take longer. For example, an architecture courses can run for five years. And to earn an undergraduate degree in the UK, you would need to complete 360 academic credits from a university or a higher education institute.

  • Academic credit

    Academic credit is the value assigned to a course or some other form of learning that counts toward program or credential completion (McQuarrie, 2016). Academic credit is used as a tool to compare learning achieved in different contexts. Credit is awarded to you when you have shown, through assessment, that you have successfully completed a module or a program by meeting the specific set of learning outcomes for that module or program (QAA, 2009).

    Within the UK, one credit represents 10 notional hours of learning. Institutions use this guide as a basis for setting the credit value of a module or program before it is offered to students. For example, a module that is estimated to involve 150 notional hours of learning will be assigned 15 credits and one that involves 400 notional hours of learning will be assigned 40 credits. In the UK, a bachelor’s degree with honors is awarded to students who achieve 360 credits (QAA, 2009).

  • Requirements for admission at an undergraduate course

    Requirements for an undergraduate admission varies from course to course and university basis. In order to take up the offer of a place to study in a course, all applicants must meet the qualification requirements of their course. The standard requirement to apply for an undergraduate course in can be as follows:

    • Fill the University online application form
    • Provide personal statement stating your plant of study, ambition, career plan
    • Provide a Curriculum Vitae (CV)
    • Academic qualification including transcript and certification of completion of (A-level) for hom
    • Evidence of English language proficiency (IELTS) test result. The score varies on course to course, but it is ideal that most of the university would expect at lest 6 or 6.5 to accept you for an undergraduate degree.
    • 2x Recommendations form your school/high school teacher or other professionals. If you are a mature student, you may need this recommendation from your employer.
    • If you are international student, you would also need to provide your or your parents bank account statement to proof your eligibility to finance the course.
    • A copy of your passport (other form of acceptable) identification. Most of the university prefer passport.
    • If you have any other professional qualification, you might have to provide that too to assess your application.
    • Attend admission interview and sit for admission test (if you are asked by the university)
  • Estimated cost to attend an undergraduate course in the UK

    There is no specific answer to identify the exact cost of completing an undergraduate course. The cost of completing an undergraduate course depends on what course and university you would choose to attend to. But generally speaking, most universities charge higher fees for international students in comparison to home students.

    For international students, an estimated cost for academic year 2021 on an undergraduate program can be between £14,000 to £16,00 per year excluding the cost of accommodation. The estimated accommodation cost can be between £800 to £1000 per month. But this cost of tuition and accommodation varies based on course, university, city, and area. To learn more about the costs, book an appointment to speak to one of our expert consultants.

  • English proficiency requirement

    Usually you can not have an admission to a course unless you can prove that you have the sufficient level of knowledge in English language. Most universities would ask you to take IELTS exam and score at least 6 or 6.5. There are courses that require an even higher score.

    If you do not have IELTS, you might prove English proficiency with other English efficiency certificates such as an English Medium course, English certificates that cover the required level of English for the particular course you are applying for, TOEFL etc.

Pre-Master’s Courses

This course is run as a graduate preparation programme (also known as Pre-Master’s pathway). The curriculum is equipped to help students gain entry to a master’s degree. Many students do not qualify for admission on a master’s degree. Attending this course can help them to gain knowledge and skills to access to a full master’s degree. It can also be regarded as a preparatory course.

Most of the Pre-Master’s course run for a short period (typically between 4 and 18 months), depending on the course and university and English requirement to access to the course. If you do not have the required level of English to pursue to course, your course duration is likely to be longer as you may have to attend additional lessons to get into the course.

  • Cost of studying in the UK

    Pre-Master’s course tuition fees are typically between £13,000 and £15,000 depending on what subject area and what course you would take. You can speak to our consultants for more information about the costs involved in a pathway course in the UK.

  • Entry requirements

    Entry requirements vary but typical requirements are:

    • At least 3 years of higher education or equivalent
    • English language: IELTS 4.5 to 5.5 or accepted equivalent
    • Your personal statement with details of your purpose of study, aspiration, and future career goal upon completion of your study.
    • Your identification (copy of your passport), or other form of national ID. Most universities prefer passport
    • If you are seeking entry to the pathway course based on your long-term work experience, you would need to provide evidence of working in capacity of Manager of an organization or a department

      • There are many pathway courses available in our partners universities, if you would like to learn more abut the admission on a course, please contact us and our expert team of admission consultant would guide you for an admission onto a suitable pathway course leading to your master’s degree.

Postgraduate Degree

Post graduate course is a degree that student’s study after they have graduated with a bachelor’s degree.  Basically, student need to have completed a bachelor’s degree before embarking on a post graduate degree (there are some exceptions to this basic rules). There are four types of post graduate degree available such as taught course, research degree, conversion, and professional qualification.

  • Postgraduate Taught courses

    There are two types of taught courses such as master’s degree and postgraduate diploma or certificate. The duration of a taught master’s course is usually over one or two years and includes a dissertation or project in most subjects.

    • Taught Master’s courses can be a Master of the Art (MA), a Master of Science (MSc), a Master of Business Administration (MBA) and a Master of Engineering (Meng) degree.
    • Postgraduate diploma or certificate are academic or vocational qualifications. A post graduate diploma course takes around 9 months to one year whereas the certificate course can take six months.
    • There are opportunities to study integrated master’s degree, which form part of undergraduate degree. It also important to know that not every master’s degree is fully taught degree. For example, a student can do a Master of Research degree, which can focus on around independent research. Although, a Master of Research degree is still consider/count as a taught course, 60 % of the course focus on independent individual research project.
  • Entry requirement for a Postgraduate Taught course

    Entry requirement for postgraduate master’s degree vary based on subject area and course that are chosen for study. Some of the example of entry requirement are:

    • Certificate and transcript of compilation of a bachelor’s degree
    • Evidence of English proficiency certificate (unless first degree was taught in English). Most university would expect IELTS score between 6.5 to 7 with each component not below 5.5.
    • Personal statement with full details of purpose of study, future ambition, and career plan upon graduation with the degree

  • Conversion courses (postgraduate)

    Conversion courses are usually one-year taught courses and are often heavily vocational. There are different levels of conversion courses: certificate, diploma, and masters.

    This course is for those who studied different subject in undergraduate but would like to study other discipline without having any previous background. This course can also be helpful for you, if you come to a point where you think your chosen career is not right for you and would like to convert it to a different subject, you can think about conversion course. For example, a law conversion course or a Graduate Diploma in Law [GDL] offers people who did not study law at undergraduate level to get a foot in the door of their chosen career in law. Equally, you can do conversion courses in other subjects, including psychology, social work, business and I.T.

  • Entry requirement for a postgraduate Conversion courses

    Entry requirement for postgraduate Conversion courses also vary based on subject area and course that are chosen for study. Some of the example of entry requirement are:

    • Certificate and transcript of compilation of a bachelor’s degree
    • Evidence of English proficiency certificate (unless first degree was taught in English). Most university would expect IELTS score between 6.5 to 7 with each component not below 5.5.
    • Personal statement with full details of purpose of study, future ambition, and career plan upon graduation with the degree
  • Estimated cost of a conversion courses.

    The estimated cost for a conversion course can be between £14,000 to 16,000 for international students. The cost for home students (UK) can be between £12,000 to £16,000. The cost of tuition changes every year and if you would like to know exact cost of conversion program in a particular subject area, please talk to one of our expert consultants

  • Professional qualifications

    There are many professional qualifications offered by universities and other professional bodies. These professional qualifications are basically entry qualifications for various careers. For example, if you want to be a solicitor, you will have to take legal practice course (LPC). The professional courses offer practical training and predominantly focuses on providing entry into a profession or allow you to develop your career further on an existing job.

Research degrees

Large part of postgraduate study deals with independent research. Research degrees are often referred as doctorates. The main type of doctorate degrees are PhDs, DPhils, integrated PhDs, and professional doctorates

  • Doctorate programs

    Doctorate programs can be taken after completing a master’s degree. In some case doctorate degree can be taken right after the bachelor’s degree during which a master is earned along the way. A doctorate degree in the UK usually takes three to five years

    The most important component of a PhD degree is the doctoral thesis. The thesis is research project on a special topic and can be between 40,000 and 12,000 words. The doctorate thesis needs to be worthy of publication and add something new to the field of study for a student to earn a PhD degree.

  • Academic entry qualification overview
  • MPhil programme

    An MPhil program is very much similar to a PhD program, but it is lower in academic packing order. For MPhil degree it is expected to complete a dissertation of between 30,000 to 35,000 words. It is still well accepted and respected in the academic and professional world, but student do not get the title of Doctor with an MPhil degree.
